Locations

Ōrākei

Wharf

Ōrākei Wharf offers easy access and scenic views of the Waitemata Harbour. Snapper and kahawai are commonly caught here, making it ideal for casual and beginner anglers. Tidy and well-maintained, it's a great option for urban fishing.

Waiheke Island

Waiheke Island offers a more remote and peaceful fishing experience. Its rocky shorelines and wharves attract snapper, John Dory, and kingfish. Be sure to follow local rules as it is a special environment worth preserving for future generations.

Find on Map

Murrays Bay Wharf

Located along the North Shore, Murrays Bay Wharf is known for calm waters and a relaxed vibe. It's a great spot for catching sprats, mackerel, and trevally. A great location that supports responsible shoreline fishing.

Find on Map

Auckland Harbour

At the city's waterfront, Auckland Harbour offers diverse fishing opportunities. From snapper to kingfish, the variety is strong, but so are the regulations. Stick to legal zones and enjoy an iconic fishing experience.

Find on Map

Devonport Wharf

With stunning views of the harbour and Rangitoto, Devonport Wharf is an amazing and accessible spot. It's ideal for catching small fish like yellowtail and jack mackerel, perfect for beginners learning to fish.

Find on Map

Birkenhead Wharf

Conveniently located with ferry access, Birkenhead Wharf is a local favourite for evening fishing. Anglers often reel in snapper and baitfish such as pilchards on this wharf. A reliable legal spot just minutes from central Auckland.

Find on Map

Point England Reserve

Set against grassy reserves and open space, Point England is perfect for land-based fishing. Kahawai and mullet are frequent catches here. This east-side gem supports community-led efforts to fish legally and sustainably.

Find on Map

Māngere Bridge

Māngere Bridge offers a blend of strong tidal flows and fish-rich waters. It's a hot spot for kahawai and eels, with plenty of space for land-based fishing. A southern location is known for its community and conservation focus.
